Polyaspartic/Polyurea vs. Epoxy

Polyaspartic/polyurea concrete coatings are superior to epoxy in New England's climate due to their enhanced flexibility, faster cure times, and superior UV resistance. Unlike epoxy, which can yellow, crack, or delaminate with temperature swings and moisture, polyaspartic/polyurea systems are engineered to withstand freeze-thaw cycles, humidity, and harsh winter conditions. This makes them ideal for garages, basements, and outdoor surfaces that face New England’s unpredictable weather year-round.

What is Polyaspartic/polyurea?

Polyaspartic/polyurea coating is a high-performance concrete coating system designed to protect and enhance concrete surfaces. Originally developed for industrial applications, this advanced technology offers superior durability, chemical resistance, and fast cure times compared to traditional coatings like epoxy.


Polyurea forms the base, providing extreme flexibility and strength, while polyaspartic acts as the topcoat, offering UV stability and a smooth, attractive finish. Together, they create a seamless, non-porous surface that resists stains, abrasions, and hot tire pickup. It's an ideal solution for garages, basements, patios, and commercial floors—especially in demanding environments like New England where temperature swings and moisture are common.
